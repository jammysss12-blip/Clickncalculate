What is Area?

Area is the measure of space inside a flat (two-dimensional) surface. It is usually expressed in square units such as square meters (m²), square centimeters (cm²), square feet (ft²), and square inches (in²).

Area Formulas Explained

Shape Formula Description Example
Square A = a² All sides equal length 5² = 25 cm²
Rectangle A = l × w Length × width 8 × 4 = 32 cm²
Triangle A = (b × h) / 2 Base × height ÷ 2 (6 × 4) / 2 = 12 cm²
Circle A = π × r² Pi × radius squared π × 3² ≈ 28.26 cm²
Parallelogram A = b × h Base × height 6 × 4 = 24 cm²
Trapezium A = (a + b) / 2 × h Average of parallel sides × height (4 + 6) / 2 × 3 = 15 cm²

📐 Area Formulas for Common Shapes

1. Square

Formula: Area = side × side

Example: Side = 5 cm, Area = 5 × 5 = 25 cm²

2. Rectangle

Formula: Area = length × width

Example: Length = 8, Width = 4, Area = 8 × 4 = 32 cm²

3. Triangle

Formula: Area = (base × height) / 2

Example: Base = 6, Height = 4, Area = (6 × 4) / 2 = 12 cm²

4. Circle

Formula: Area = π × r²

Example: Radius = 3, Area ≈ 3.14 × 9 = 28.26 cm²
