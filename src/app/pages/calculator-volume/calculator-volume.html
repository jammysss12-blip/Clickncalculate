What is Volume?

Volume is the amount of space inside a three-dimensional object. It is measured in cubic units such as cubic meters (m³), cubic centimeters (cm³), liters (L), and cubic feet (ft³).

Volume Formulas Explained

Shape Formula Description Example
Cube V = a³ All sides equal length 4³ = 64 cm³
Rectangular Prism V = l × w × h Length × width × height 5 × 3 × 2 = 30 cm³
Cylinder V = π × r² × h Circular base × height π × 3² × 5 ≈ 141.3 cm³
Sphere V = (4/3) × π × r³ Perfectly round object (4/3) × π × 3³ ≈ 113.1 cm³
Cone V = (1/3) × π × r² × h Circular base, tapers to point (1/3) × π × 3² × 5 ≈ 47.1 cm³
Pyramid V = (1/3) × base area × h Base and triangular sides (1/3) × 25 × 6 = 50 cm³

📐 Volume Formulas for Common Shapes

1. Cube

Formula: Volume = a³

Where: a = side length

Example: If side = 4 cm, Volume = 4 × 4 × 4 = 64 cm³

2. Rectangular Prism (Box)

Formula: Volume = length × width × height

Example: Length = 5, Width = 3, Height = 2, Volume = 5 × 3 × 2 = 30 cm³

3. Cylinder

Formula: Volume = π × r² × h

Where: r = radius, h = height

Example: Radius = 3, Height = 5, Volume ≈ 3.14 × 9 × 5 = 141.3 cm³
