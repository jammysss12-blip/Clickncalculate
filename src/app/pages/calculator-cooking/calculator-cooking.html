📏 Common Conversion Units

1. Volume Units

  • • Cups – standard US or metric cup
  • • Tablespoons (Tbsp) – 1 cup = 16 Tbsp
  • • Teaspoons (Tsp) – 1 Tbsp = 3 Tsp
  • • Milliliters (ml) – 1 cup ≈ 240 ml
  • • Liters (L) – 1000 ml = 1 L

2. Weight Units

  • • Grams (g) – metric unit
  • • Kilograms (kg) – 1000 g = 1 kg
  • • Ounces (oz) – 1 oz ≈ 28.35 g
  • • Pounds (lb) – 1 lb = 16 oz

🥄 Common Ingredient Conversions

  • • 1 cup flour ≈ 125 g
  • • 1 cup sugar ≈ 200 g
  • • 1 cup butter ≈ 227 g
  • • 1 tbsp = 15 ml
  • • 1 tsp = 5 ml

Note: Different ingredients have different densities, so weight-to-volume conversion varies.

� Example Usage

  • • Convert 2 cups of sugar to grams: 2 × 200 g = 400 g
  • • Convert 3 tablespoons of olive oil to ml: 3 × 15 ml = 45 ml
  • • Convert 500 g flour to cups: 500 ÷ 125 g = 4 cups

How many grams is 1 cup of flour?

1 cup of all-purpose flour is approximately 120–125 grams. The exact weight can vary slightly depending on how the flour is measured — scooped flour weighs more than spooned flour. Use our Cooking Converter for precise results.

How many tablespoons are in a cup?

There are 16 tablespoons in 1 cup. Similarly, 1 tablespoon equals 3 teaspoons, and 1 cup equals 48 teaspoons.

Why do cups and grams give different values for different ingredients?

A cup measures volume, while grams measure weight. Different ingredients have different densities — 1 cup of water weighs 240g, but 1 cup of flour weighs only 120g. That is why weight measurements like grams are more accurate for baking.
