üìö Loan Calculator Guide

üí∞ About Amortized Loans

  • Fixed Payments: Equal monthly installments
  • Interest Front-loaded: Early payments are mostly interest
  • Equity Building: Principal reduces with each payment
  • Amortization Schedule: Detailed payment breakdown
  • Prepayment Options: May reduce total interest

üè† Common Loan Types

  • Mortgages: 15-30 year terms, secured by property
  • Auto Loans: 3-7 year terms, secured by vehicle
  • Personal Loans: 1-7 year terms, unsecured
  • Student Loans: 10-25 year terms, education financing

‚è≥ About Deferred Payments

  • Grace Period: No payments during deferment
  • Interest Accrual: Interest continues to accumulate
  • Capitalization: Interest may be added to principal
  • Extended Terms: Longer repayment after deferment

üìä Loan Impact

  • Credit Score: Affects interest rates
  • Debt-to-Income: Key approval factor
  • Loan-to-Value: Collateral requirements
  • Payment History: Affects future credit
  • Prepayment Penalties: Check terms

üìà Interest Rate Factors

  • Credit Score: Higher score = lower rates
  • Loan Term: Shorter terms = lower rates
  • Loan Amount: Larger loans may have better rates
  • Down Payment: More down = better rates
  • Market Conditions: Follows Fed rates

üí≥ Loan Costs

  • APR: Annual Percentage Rate
  • Origination Fees: 0.5-1% of loan amount
  • Prepayment Penalties: Check terms
  • Late Fees: Typically 3-5% of payment

üìÖ Loan Terms

  • Short-term: 1-3 years
  • Medium-term: 3-7 years
  • Long-term: 10-30+ years
  • Balloon: Large final payment

üìà About Bond Yields

  • Current Yield: Annual income / Market price
  • Yield to Maturity: Total return if held to maturity
  • Yield to Call: If called by issuer
  • Yield to Worst: Worst-case scenario return

üåê Bond Markets

  • Government: Treasury bonds, bills, notes
  • Corporate: Investment grade to junk
  • Municipal: Tax-exempt, local projects
  • International: Sovereign and corporate

üìä Bond Strategies

  • Laddering: Stagger maturities
  • Barbell: Short and long-term mix
  • Bullet: All bonds mature same year
  • Active vs Passive: Managed or index funds
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: Offset capital gains
