What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" means "per hundred."

For example:

  • 50% means 50 out of 100
  • 25% means 25 out of 100
  • 10% means 10 out of 100

Percentages help compare values and understand proportions quickly.

Basic Percentage Formula

The most common formula for calculating a percentage is:

Percentage = (Part ÷ Total) × 100

This formula is used in many situations, such as calculating marks, profit, or ratios.

Example: Calculating Percentage

If you scored 45 marks out of 50:

Percentage = (45 ÷ 50) × 100 = 90%

How to Find Percentage of a Number

To find a percentage of a number, use this formula:

Value = (Percentage ÷ 100) × Total Number

Example: Finding Percentage of a Number

Find 20% of 200:

20 ÷ 100 × 200 = 40
So, 20% of 200 is 40

How to Calculate Percentage Increase

Percentage increase is used when a value grows over time, such as salary increase or price rise.

Percentage Increase = [(New Value − Old Value) ÷ Old Value] × 100

Example: Percentage Increase

Old price = $100

New price = $120

Increase = $120 − $100 = $20
Percentage Increase = (20 ÷ 100) × 100 = 20%

How to Calculate Percentage Decrease

Percentage decrease is used when a value reduces, such as discounts or price drops.

Percentage Decrease = [(Old Value − New Value) ÷ Old Value] × 100

Example: Percentage Decrease

Old price = $200

New price = $150

Decrease = $200 − $150 = $50
Percentage Decrease = (50 ÷ 200) × 100 = 25%

How to Calculate Discount Percentage

Discounts are commonly used in shopping. To calculate the discount percentage:

Discount % = [(Original Price − Sale Price) ÷ Original Price] × 100

Example: Discount Percentage

Original price = $100

Sale price = $80

Discount = $100 − $80 = $20
Discount % = (20 ÷ 100) × 100 = 20%

Quick Tricks to Calculate Percentage Mentally

You don't always need a calculator. Here are some simple tricks:

1. Find 10% Easily

Example: 10% of 250 = 25

2. Find 5%
